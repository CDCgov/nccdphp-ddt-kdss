Advanced Kidney Disease is Equally as Prevalent among Individuals with Prediabetes as those with Undiagnosed Diabetes

September 2022

Prediabetes affects 96 million individuals in the US (38% of the adult population)1. While prediabetes may be reversible, over time it can progress to Type 2 Diabetes, which affects nearly every major organ in the body, including kidneys, blood vessels, nerves, and the eyes.

Examining data from the National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey (NHANES, 1999-2018), reveals the prevalence of advanced kidney disease (stages 3-4) is approximately 5% for individuals without diabetes, over 20% for individuals with diagnosed diabetes, and varies between 10-14% for both individuals with prediabetes and those with undiagnosed diabetes.

Lifestyle factors, including exercise, weight loss, and healthy eating, can reverse prediabetes. Doctors can prescribe exercise regimens as a type of ‘medication.’ Healthy eating means moderate intake of sodium and low intake of refined sugar. There are many programs to help support lifestyle changes. Improving lifestyle among individuals with prediabetes could save millions of lives and dollars.
