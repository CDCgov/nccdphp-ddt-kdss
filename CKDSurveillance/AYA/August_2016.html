Number of Nephrologists for Patients with Chronic Kidney Disease in the United States

According to the American Medical Association (AMA), approximately 9,000 US physicians claimed nephrology (kidney disease) as their specialty in 2013. This was almost double the number in 1999.

Note: Adapted from the AMA.

The yearly growth in the number of nephrologists (kidney doctors) suggests that the medical community is recognizing and addressing the need to care for people with chronic kidney disease (CKD). In 2011-2014, in the National Health Examination and Nutrition Survey (NHANES), about 15%-16% or 31-34 million of the US population aged 20 years or older had CKD stages 1-4 (not on dialysis).* About 50% of this group (8%-9%, 15-18 million) had evidence of advanced CKD (stage 3 or 4). Thus, there were approximately 1,600-2,000 patients with advanced CKD per nephrologist in 2013. Despite the rapid increase in the number of nephrologists, the ratio of patients per nephrologist remains high, and emphasizes the important role of primary care providers in caring for people with CKD.

* Prevalence estimates were obtained from NHANES using the Modification of Diet in Renal Disease (MDRD) Study equation.
