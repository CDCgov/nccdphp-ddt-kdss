Diagnosis of High Blood Pressure Is Declining Among Adults With Chronic Kidney Disease

May 2019

High blood pressure (hypertension) is a common cause of chronic kidney disease (CKD) and one of the leading causes of kidney failure. In the United States, approximately 1 in 3 adults, or 75 million people, have high blood pressure.1 Data from OPTUM Clinformatics show that among adult patients enrolled in commercial insurance, the percentage with a diagnostic code for high blood pressure* increased with age. From 2006 to 2016, the percentage peaked in 2010 for those aged 30–64 years and then declined. Among patients aged 20–29 years, the percentage with a diagnostic code for high blood pressure was the same in 2006 as in 2016. Continued awareness and support of high blood pressure prevention and education efforts are important to prevent or delay CKD and CKD progression.

*High blood pressure was determined using International Classification of Diseases—Clinical Modification (9th and 10th revisions) diagnostic codes in claims from OPTUM Clinformatics. OPTUM Clinformatics provides paid medical and prescription claims and enrollment information for participants in the commercial insurance plans and Medicare Advantage plans of a large US managed-care health insurance company.
