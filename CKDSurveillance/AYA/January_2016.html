Awareness of Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) in the United States Is Low

Currently 13%-15% of the US population have CKD based on estimates from the National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey (NHANES).

However, even in advanced CKD (eGFR 15-29), only about half of NHANES participants were aware of having CKD.

eGFR or estimated glomerular filtration rate is a measure of kidney function; it is the rate at which the kidneys filter wastes and extra fluid from the blood measured in milliliters per minute. People with kidney damage (e.g., having albumin or protein in the urine, also known as microalbuminuria or macroalbuminuria depending on the amount of albumin or protein in the urine) or with eGFR less than 60 for 3 months or longer are considered to have CKD. The lower the eGFR, the more advanced is the stage of the disease.
