Older Adults with Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) Report More Disability than Older Adults Without CKD

In the United States, disabilities and limitations in physical activity are reported more often by older adults with CKD than by older adults without CKD. Compared with older adults without CKD, adults aged 65 years or older with CKD reported more limitations due to confusion and more limitations in their ability to work, in the type or amount of work performed, in walking, and in their ability to perform a variety of activities. In terms of activities of daily living (e.g., eating, dressing), instrumental activities of daily living (e.g., managing money, preparing meals), and leisure and social activities, the reported disability was 20%–33% greater in older people with CKD than among older adults without CKD. Older adults with more advanced stages of CKD (stages 3 and 4) had similar disability as older adults with earlier stages (stages 1 and 2). Continued research and awareness towards identifying and understanding the underlying causes of disability among older people with CKD may assist in developing and targeting interventions to reduce such disability.

Note: ADL, activities of daily living (getting in and out of bed, using fork, knife, or cup for eating, dressing oneself); IADL, instrumental activities of daily living (managing money, household chores, preparing meals); LSA, leisure and social activities (going to the movies, attending social event, leisure activity at home); LEM, lower extremity mobility (walking ¼ mile, walking up 10 steps, stooping, kneeling, or crouching, walking between rooms on same floor, standing up from armless chair): GPA, general physical activity (lifting or carrying heavy objects, reaching up overhead, grasping or holding small objects, standing for long periods, sitting for long periods).

People with kidney damage (those having albumin in the urine) or with an estimated glomerular filtration rate-a measure of kidney function-less than 60 mL/min per 1.73 m2 for 3 months or longer are considered to have CKD. CKD has various levels or stages of seriousness. The higher the stage, the more advanced the disease.
