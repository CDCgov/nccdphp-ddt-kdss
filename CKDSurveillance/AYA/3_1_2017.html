Long-term Trends in the Prevalence of Obesity in the United States

Obesity has been linked with both diabetes and high blood pressure, two major risk factors for chronic kidney disease (CKD).1,2 Data from the National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey, a nationally representative survey,3 show that the prevalence of obesity among adults aged 20 years or older increased significantly between 1999 and 2014 (from 30% to 38%). This trend highlights the importance of continued public health action to reduce the prevalence of obesity in the US adult population and help lower the risk of developing CKD.

 

Footnote: Prevalence estimates for US adults aged 20 years or older and unadjusted for age.
