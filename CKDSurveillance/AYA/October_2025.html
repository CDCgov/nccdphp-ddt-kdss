Provider Awareness of Chronic Kidney Disease in the Military Health System

August 2025

Lack of provider awareness of chronic kidney disease (CKD) leads to delays in effective therapies to prevent cardiovascular complications and kidney failure. Trends in provider awareness of advanced CKD (stages 3–5) were assessed in the U.S. Military Health System from 2009 to 2023. Provider awareness was defined as presence of ICD diagnosis codes in the electronic health record among beneficiaries with an estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR) below 60 ml/min/1.73m2. The crude prevalence of beneficiaries with CKD stages 3–5 who also had an ICD-9 diagnosis code was 25.0%–36.5% in 2009–2015. After conversion to ICD-10 in 2016, the overall crude prevalence was 30.0%–36.0%. Provider awareness in 2023 was very high (76%) for stages 4 and 5 but was only 32% for stage 3.

The poor documentation of CKD stage 3 indicates a target for improvement. Low provider recognition of CKD has also been reported for other healthcare systems in the United States. 1The factors contributing to low provider awareness are not clear. A concerted approach including provider education and more effective use of clinical support systems may be necessary.2
